  • Euphoria

  • Love/Hate
  • Elation wears off and we realize the limitations of that technology

  • A lot of VR development wasn’t ready for the long tail of adoption and went out of business

How does technology become domesticated?

  • Value
  • Friction

Technology that doesn’t produce enough value to overcome its inherent friction will never be domesticated

We're currently in love/hate w/ VR.

  • Value
  • We mostly have full lives, so what does the device replace?
  • Friction
  • How easy is it to pick up and use?
  • Cost, Ubiquity, Ease of Use

3D TV

  • Value: A more engaging way to watch a movie
  • Friction: 3D glasses
  • Verdict: Gimmick

Mobile Phones

  • Value: Communicate Anywhere
  • Friction: battery life, size, cost, signal
  • Phones addressed all of their initial issues

Virtual Reality

  • Value: Presence and Agency
  • Friction: optics, cost, UX, wires, space
  • A lot of companies are working on solving these problems: Facebook, Google, Apple, Sony, Valve
